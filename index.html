Common Phishing Techniques

Understanding different phishing methods is the first step in protecting yourself from cyber threats.

Email Phishing

Fraudulent emails that appear to be from reputable sources to steal sensitive data like login credentials and credit card information.

Smishing

Phishing attacks conducted via SMS text messages, often containing malicious links or requests for personal information.

Vishing

Voice phishing where attackers use phone calls to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information or performing actions.

How to Protect Yourself

Follow these essential steps to significantly reduce your risk of falling victim to phishing attacks.

1

Verify Email Senders

Always check the sender's email address carefully. Look for subtle misspellings or domain changes that indicate a fake address.

2

Hover Before You Click

Hover your mouse over any links in emails to see the actual URL before clicking. If it looks suspicious, don't click.

3

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication

Use MFA wherever possible. Even if your credentials are stolen, attackers won't be able to access your accounts without the second factor.

4

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, browsers, and security software to protect against known vulnerabilities.
